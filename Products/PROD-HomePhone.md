## Overview

ClearWave Home Phone is a VoIP (Voice over IP) landline service delivered over ClearWave's full-fibre broadband connection. It replaces traditional copper-line telephony and is available as an add-on to any ClearWave fibre broadband product. Customers retain their existing landline number and continue to use standard DECT or corded handsets via an analogue telephone adapter (ATA).

> **Important:** This is a VoIP service — it requires an active ClearWave broadband connection. It will not work during a broadband outage or power cut unless the customer has a backup power supply. Agents must communicate this clearly at point of sale.

## Key Features

- Unlimited UK landline and mobile calls included (fair use: up to 5,000 minutes/month)
- Caller ID included
- Voicemail included (accessible via app or 1571)
- Number porting: existing landline number can be retained
- Compatible with existing DECT cordless handsets via the included analogue telephone adapter (ATA)
- Emergency calls (999/112) supported — with limitations (see Regulatory Considerations)
- Available as an add-on to any ClearWave fibre broadband plan

## Eligibility

- Must hold an active ClearWave fibre broadband plan (any tier)
- Not available as a standalone service
- Number porting available for BT Openreach and most other UK landline providers

## Regulatory Considerations

- **Emergency calls**: VoIP home phone services must support 999/112. However, unlike traditional copper lines, the service will not work during a power outage or broadband failure unless the customer has an uninterruptible power supply (UPS). Agents must provide the **Backup for Voice** leaflet and verbally explain this limitation at point of sale — this is an **Ofcom regulatory requirement**.
- Customers who rely on their landline for medical alarms, telecare devices, or monitored alarms must be advised that the ATA may not be compatible with these devices — refer to the compatibility check procedure before sale.
- Number porting: agents must not guarantee a specific porting completion date; the standard timeframe is 5–10 working days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will my existing cordless phones work?**
A: Yes, provided they are standard DECT handsets. The ATA plugs into the router and provides a standard analogue phone socket. Simply plug the base station into the ATA rather than the wall socket.

**Q: What happens to my landline if the internet goes down?**
A: Because this is a VoIP service, the home phone will not work if the broadband connection is down. Customers should keep a mobile phone charged for emergencies. A UPS device can maintain the router and ATA during short power cuts.

**Q: Can I keep my existing number?**
A: Yes in most cases. Provide the current provider name and the existing number; the port usually completes within 5–10 working days. The customer keeps their old service active until porting is confirmed.

**Q: Are calls to mobiles included?**
A: Yes — the unlimited calls allowance includes UK mobile numbers as well as landlines.
