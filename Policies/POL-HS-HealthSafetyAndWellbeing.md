## Purpose

Provide a safe, healthy working environment and actively support the mental and physical wellbeing of all team members.

## Legal Framework

- Health and Safety at Work Act 1974
- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
- Display Screen Equipment (DSE) Regulations 1992

## Physical Health — Workstation Standards

All team members working at a fixed workstation must have:
- A DSE assessment completed on joining and whenever workstation setup changes
- An adjustable chair and monitor at a comfortable height
- A headset assessed for comfort and volume levels (prolonged exposure to loud callers must be reported)
- Regular breaks away from the screen — at minimum the allocated scheduled breaks

Concerns about workstation comfort must be raised with the Team Leader; a DSE reassessment will be arranged within 5 working days.

## Voice Health

Call centre work presents specific risks to voice health:
- Team members must have access to water at all times during shift
- Voice strain symptoms (persistent hoarseness, pain when speaking) must be reported to the Team Leader and referred to Occupational Health
- Where a voice condition is medically confirmed, reasonable adjustments (e.g. reduced call time, written channel support) must be explored

## Mental Health & Wellbeing

- **Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)**: Confidential counselling and support available to all team members — details posted in the team area and intranet
- **Mental Health First Aiders**: At least one trained Mental Health First Aider on each shift; names published in each team area
- **Stress Risk Assessments**: Required where a team member or manager identifies work-related stress as a concern; actioned within 10 working days
- **Wellbeing check-ins**: Team Leaders are expected to hold a brief wellbeing check-in (separate from KPI review) in each monthly 1:1

## Reporting Incidents

- All accidents, near-misses, and work-related health concerns must be reported in the incident log on the day they occur
- RIDDOR-reportable incidents (see guidance in the safety notice board) must be reported to the Health & Safety lead within 24 hours

## Lone Working (Remote / Home-Based Agents)

- Remote agents are covered by the same DSE and wellbeing obligations
- Managers must include remote wellbeing check-ins as part of their regular 1:1 schedule
- Remote team members must report workstation concerns to HR; adjustments will be supported where reasonable
