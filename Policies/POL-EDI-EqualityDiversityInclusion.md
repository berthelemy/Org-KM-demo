## Purpose
Foster an inclusive workplace where every team member is treated with dignity and respect, and where diversity is actively valued.

## Legal Framework
- Equality Act 2010 — nine protected characteristics:
  1. Age
  2. Disability
  3. Gender reassignment
  4. Marriage and civil partnership
  5. Pregnancy and maternity
  6. Race
  7. Religion or belief
  8. Sex
  9. Sexual orientation

## Key Commitments
- Zero tolerance for discrimination, harassment, or victimisation on any of the protected characteristics
- Reasonable adjustments made for team members with disabilities or health conditions
- Equitable access to training, development, and promotion opportunities
- Inclusive recruitment practices — job-related criteria only, structured interviews, diverse shortlists

## Harassment & Bullying

### Definitions
- **Harassment**: Unwanted conduct related to a protected characteristic that violates a person's dignity or creates an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment
- **Bullying**: Persistent offensive, abusive, intimidating, or insulting behaviour that makes the recipient feel threatened, humiliated, or vulnerable
- **Third-party harassment**: Harassment of a team member by a customer; managers must protect team members from repeated third-party harassment

### Reporting Routes
1. Speak to Team Leader or skip-level manager
2. Contact HR directly (confidential)
3. Use the anonymous speak-up/whistleblowing channel (details on intranet)
4. Raise a formal Grievance (see [[POL-DISC-DisciplinaryAndGrievance]])

Team members who report harassment will be protected from victimisation.

## Reasonable Adjustments
Requests for reasonable adjustments must be:
- Assessed individually, in consultation with Occupational Health where appropriate
- Implemented within a reasonable timeframe (target: 10 working days for straightforward adjustments)
- Documented in the HR system
- Reviewed periodically as circumstances change

## Manager Responsibilities
- Complete EDI e-learning on appointment to a management role and annually thereafter
- Intervene promptly when they witness or are made aware of discriminatory behaviour
- Ensure team communications and practices are inclusive
- Never make decisions about promotion, workload, or development on the basis of a protected characteristic
